About the election

DIF was established to represent our fast changing community and to create a safe space for the co-development of next-generation code and specifications. From the original handful of members 5 years ago, the foundation has grown to become an organization with over 300 member companies, representing thousands of PRs and a strong commitment to decentralizing identity software.

Since its inception, DIF has been primarily governed by a Steering Committee. The Steering Committee is an elected group of member representatives whom lead DIF, set strategy, assure the high quality of ratified work items, react to the communityâ€™s needs, among other decisions. Elections occur each year to ensure not only a democratic process to lead and administer DIF, but to continually provide a diverse and inclusive mix of commitee members, representative of the fabric of DIF's community.

Nomination process


The nomination process for the election is as follows :


  • ALL members can nominate any other member, or themselves!
  • All members are eligible for a seat on the Steering Committee (SC) - a comprehensive list of the mix of SC representatives is as follows :

    • The Steering Committee is comprised of 11 positions, 10 SC Members and one role for the Technical Steering Committee lead
    • At least 2 SC positions for each of the following associate member organizations, > 1000 employees, 10 - 1000 employees, 1 - 10 employees
    • The Steering Committee may have the remaining positions filled by individual contributors
  • Members may only nominate one member and only submit a nomination once (you may change this choice in the nomination window)
  • The Nominee should have some degree of organizational or coodinational skill
  • The Individual should be nominated for their personal qualifications and commitment, not solely for their company or organizational affiliation
  • Nominations open on the 6th of April 2022 - please get them in !
    •


    Voting process


    The election process adheres to the following governing rules for voting :

  • Only Associate members can vote, and each votes as a whole on one ballot. One ballot can contain up to 4 names. (one name per available seat at the Steering Committee).
  • Submission of ballots is made via a google form.
  • These will be audited confidentially and not published in any way (not even as tallies).
  • If one member organization accidentally votes more than once, DIF staff will reply to the emails to clarify the issue, so please use a monitored/normal email address in the organization's domain rather than personal accounts or "alts."
  • All ballots are assumed to be collective/company-wide decisions even if multiple are received.
  • The voting is open between 20th May and 27th May. The deadline for the last vote is 23:59 GMT on the 27th of May.
